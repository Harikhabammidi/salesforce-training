📌 What is CRM?

CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management.
It is a technology used by organizations to manage customer data, communication, sales processes, and support services in a single platform.

CRM helps businesses to:

Maintain customer information efficiently
Improve customer relationships
Track sales and business activities
Automate workflows and processes
Increase productivity and collaboration
🌟 Why Companies Use Salesforce

Salesforce Official Website

Salesforce is one of the most popular CRM platforms used worldwide. Companies use Salesforce to:

Manage customer relationships
Store business data securely
Track leads and opportunities
Improve customer support services
Automate business workflows
Generate reports and analytics
📘 Salesforce Basic Concepts
🏢 Account

An Account represents an organization, company, or institution.

Example: College

👤 Contact

A Contact represents a person associated with an Account.

Example: Student

🎯 Opportunity

An Opportunity represents a potential process, deal, or business outcome.

Example: Course Enrollment Process

🔄 CRM Business Flow
Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer
🔹 Lead

A person who shows interest in a product, service, or organization.

Example: Student asking about admission details.

🔹 Contact

A verified person whose details are stored for future communication.

Example: Student who submitted the application form.

🔹 Opportunity

A possible successful process or deal in progress.

Example: Student admission or enrollment process.

🔹 Customer

The final successful outcome after completing the process.

Example: Student successfully joined the college.
